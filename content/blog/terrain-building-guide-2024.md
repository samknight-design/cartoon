Nothing transforms a D&D session like physical terrain. Players lean in, argue over sight lines, argue over whether they can squeeze through that gap, and generally become far more engaged in the fiction. But the cost of commercial terrain can be eye-watering — £150 for a full dungeon set from major brands is not uncommon.

The good news: 3D printed terrain has democratised the hobby entirely.

## Why 3D Printed Terrain Changes Everything

FDM printing (fused filament) is perfect for terrain because:

- **Layer lines are invisible** on stone textures — they actually add to the organic look
- **PLA is cheap** — a full dungeon set might use £3–5 of filament
- **Modular systems** mean you print once, use forever
- **Scale-correct** — 5 feet per inch (1" = 5ft) works perfectly with standard 25/28mm miniatures

The only constraint was time: printing a full dungeon traditionally took days. Our service removes that barrier — order a complete modular set and have it in your hands by Saturday.

## Material Choice: FDM vs Resin for Terrain

Almost always: **FDM PLA**.

Resin is overkill for terrain — the extra cost doesn't translate into visible improvement at tabletop distances. FDM's slight layer lines actually *add* to the stone texture effect. The only exceptions:

- **Tiny detail accessories** (candles, small skulls, tiny books): use resin
- **NPC figures on terrain** (a seated innkeeper, a chained prisoner): use resin

## Painting Your Terrain in 2 Hours

You can paint an entire dungeon set in an afternoon with this speed-painting method:

### Step 1: Prime Black (15 min)
Rattle can black primer. Don't need to be precious about it — spray from multiple angles, let dry.

### Step 2: Drybrushing (45 min)
This does 80% of the work for stone:

1. Heavy drybrush of **dark grey** (Mechanicus Standard Grey or similar)
2. Medium drybrush of **mid grey** (Dawnstone)
3. Light drybrush of **light grey/off-white** (Administratum Grey)

The raised edges catch more pigment, creating instant depth.

### Step 3: Washes (20 min)
Pool **Nuln Oil** or a dark brown wash into recesses and corners. It darkens the deepest crevices, reinforcing shadow.

### Step 4: Spot Colour (20 min)
Pick one or two accent colours:
- Mossy patches: Castellan Green with Agrax wash
- Rust stains: Ryza Orange or Typhus Corrosion
- Magical runes: any bright colour you like — Guilliman Blue, Pink Horror

### Step 5: Matte Varnish (5 min)
Essential for terrain that gets handled constantly. Matte varnish protects the paint and kills any shine.

## DM's Secret Weapon

Here's a tip most DMs don't know: print terrain in the **specific colours you'll paint them**. Grey PLA tiles drybrush much faster than black PLA tiles (less grey paint needed). Bone-coloured PLA is perfect for undead crypt pieces — often needs only a quick brown wash and highlights.
