If you've ever pulled a miniature off the build plate only to find mushy detail on the face or snapped sword tips, you know the frustration of miscalibrated resin settings. ## Choosing the Right Resin

Not all resins are created equal for miniature printing. For 28mm heroic-scale figures, you want a resin with:

- **Low viscosity** — flows into thin details without bridging
- **Good elongation at break** — sword blades and fingers survive handling
- **Grey or beige colour** — easier to spot surface defects before painting

Our go-to resins for miniatures are **Elegoo ABS-Like Grey**, **Phrozen Aqua 8K Grey**, and **Siraya Tech Simple** for beginners.

## Layer Height: 0.03mm vs 0.05mm

The debate over layer height is real. Here's our verdict:

| Layer Height | Best For | Print Time |
|---|---|---|
| 0.03mm | Faces, hair, fine weapons | +60% slower |
| 0.05mm | Most miniatures | Standard |
| 0.08mm | Bases, terrain, bulk pieces | 40% faster |

For **hero models** — the character your player will use for 50+ sessions — use 0.03mm. You'll see the individual strands of hair, the engraving on armour plates, the creases in a cloak. For enemy mobs that get painted in batches, 0.05mm is perfectly fine.

## Exposure Times: The Critical Variable

Incorrect exposure time is the #1 cause of print failures. Too little and layers won't cure properly (delamination, print falls off plate). Too much and you lose fine detail as resin over-cures into surrounding areas.

**Starting point for MSLA printers with a 405nm mono LCD:**

```
Bottom layers: 8 (count)
Bottom exposure: 30–40s
Normal exposure: 2.5–3.5s
Lift speed: 60mm/min
```

Use **Chitubox** or **Lychee Slicer**'s built-in exposure finder matrix to test your specific resin on your specific printer. This single test can save hours of failed prints.

## Support Strategy for Miniatures

This is where most hobbyists struggle. Our rules:

1. **Island detection on** — never skip it
2. **Light supports for thin details** — use 0.4mm tip, 0.6mm base
3. **Medium supports for torsos/heads** — 0.6mm tip, 1.0mm base
4. **Heavy supports for large models only** — use sparingly to avoid marks

Angle your miniature 30–45° backward. This keeps the face (the most important part) printing at a clean angle with minimal support contact points.

## Post Processing: Don't Rush It

The fastest way to ruin a beautiful print is sloppy washing. Our protocol:

1. **Two-stage IPA wash**: dirty IPA for 3 minutes, clean IPA for 1 minute
2. **Air dry** for 5 minutes — never use heat at this stage
3. **UV cure** for 2–4 minutes per side (we use a UV nail lamp for small models)
4. **Light sanding** with 800 grit on support marks

Then, and only then, prime with a zenithal prime (black from below, white/grey from above). This instantly reveals any surface issues before you reach for your paints.
